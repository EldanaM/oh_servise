Welcome to Oh Service

Supporting neurodivergent children through innovative digital solutions

The Challenge

Hello! Have you ever wondered how many people with excellent neurons live in Kazakhstan? The number of children with ASD alone is 30 thousand, not to mention other children. After studying the data, our team began to wonder: "Are there enough mobile applications for learning children with neurodivergence?"

We found that there is a lack of specialized digital tools that could help this category of children in their development and social development.

Our Mission

This topic is of great relevance in the twenties of the UN development. It is aimed at the implementation of Goal 4, ensuring quality and accessible support for education for all, and Goal 10, through the social development and development of children with ASD and Down syndrome.

Goal 4: Quality Education
Goal 10: Reduced Inequality

Our solution to this problem is a cross-platform mobile application developed for teaching children with ASD and Down syndrome.

"A application for teaching children with ASD and Down syndrome, and supporting their parents."

Our Journey

So, where did we start our work? To first collect initial ideas, we used Google Forms and conducted a survey among teenagers to assess awareness of the problems of people with neurodivergence. The results of the survey confirmed our concerns: note that the solvable problems of this group of people are insufficient. This prompted us to develop the idea and implement it.

At the next stage, we consulted a psychologist. An expert opinion was a necessary condition for forming the necessary approach to the development of the application. The psychologist requires the inclusion of advice related to everyday issues in the application, since managing financial functions plays a decisive role in the development and independence of children with ASD and Down syndrome.

One of the key moments in the development process was the organization of a focus group with a portable remote control. We involved Alisher in cooperation to get feedback directly from the user. Based on the data and recommendations received, we began developing the application. This process includes searching for information on the Internet, additional consultations with psychologists and an ongoing exchange of opinions within the team.
